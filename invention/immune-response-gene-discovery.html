Introduction

This Venezuelan-American immunologist discovered genes that control immune responses, earning a Nobel Prize and advancing organ transplantation and autoimmune disease treatment.

Baruj Benacerraf was born in Caracas, Venezuela, to Sephardic Jewish parents. He became a pioneering immunologist who discovered the genes that control how the immune system responds to foreign substances.
